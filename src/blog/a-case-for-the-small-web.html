A case for the small web in a world with ai

july 25, 2025

Social media has become inundated with AI slop. a concept that was once developed for the intention of connecting, bringing together communities, and meeting new people is now filled with deepfakes, bad ai actors, and advertisements.

The numbers tell a stark story. In just two years, AI-generated content on medium has surged from 1.8% to 37%. On Quora, the jump was sharper yet--from 2% to 39%. Meanwhile Google's AI Overviews now intercept nearly 40% of clicks that would have gone to other websites, with users increasingly likely to end their browsing sessions after seeing generated summaries.

we aren't just living alongside ai anymore. we're living through it.

The conventional narrative frames this as competition—AI versus human creators, chatbots versus search engines, automation versus authenticity. But the real story is more troubling: AI isn't just creating content, it's becoming the intermediary that controls what information we consume. In this new landscape, independent websites and the "small web" aren't just nice to have—they're essential for preserving human connection and discovery in an increasingly mediated world.

the intermediation

the mechanism is subtle but systematic. ai doesn't simply compete with human-created content, it intercepts and repackages it, keeping users within controlled ecosystems
